# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common forms of cybercrime where attackers try to trick people into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. Instead of hacking systems directly, they manipulate users by pretending to be a trusted source.

Consider a situation where a college student receives a message claiming to be from their bank. The message warns that their account may be blocked unless they verify their details immediately. It also includes a link that appears genuine. Without thinking much, the student clicks on the link and is taken to a website that looks exactly like the official bank page. Believing it to be real, the student enters their login credentials and OTP.

In reality, the website is fake and controlled by cybercriminals. As soon as the information is entered, it is captured by the attacker, who can then access the student’s bank account and perform unauthorized transactions.

Phishing attacks mainly target individuals who are unaware or in a hurry, especially students and new internet users. The consequences can include financial loss, data theft, and misuse of personal identity. This highlights the importance of being cautious while interacting with unknown links or messages online.
